# Redacted Resume Site Design Brief

Design a polished personal website for a junior AI/product candidate.

Audience:
- HR, referral partners, hiring managers, and career coaches.
- The site should feel credible enough to share with a recruiter, not like a loud sales page.

Candidate positioning:
- Undergraduate student, target roles: AI product, product operations, product intern.
- Strength: can identify real user pain, design a product concept, ship with AI-assisted development, and measure early traction.

Public proof points:
- Built an AI food recommendation mini app from idea to launch in one week.
- Built an international game utility website and mini program with Next.js and Supabase.
- The game utility reached 2,280+ unique visitors, 3,170+ page views, and 200+ UGC items in the first month.
- Participated in an ESG scoring product project covering user segmentation, pricing, scoring model, and prototype.
- Internship experience in finance and content operations.

Desired website structure:
- Hero: name, one-line role, one-line differentiation, primary CTA.
- Proof section: 2-3 strongest projects with outcomes and decisions.
- Experience section: finance internship, content operations internship, product project work.
- Skills section: AI collaboration, product thinking, Supabase, Figma, Next.js, data/content analysis.
- Contact/share section.

Design direction:
- Modern, refined, recruiter-readable.
- More like a high-quality interactive portfolio than a resume pasted onto a page.
- Avoid generic AI UI: purple gradients, excessive cards, fake logos, fake testimonials, stock imagery, oversized centered headline.
- Use strong typography, clear hierarchy, mobile-first layout, and a few meaningful interactions.

Privacy constraints:
- Do not include private contact number, private contact details, full resume text, exact school ID, or fake third-party endorsements.
- Do not invent metrics beyond the proof points above.
